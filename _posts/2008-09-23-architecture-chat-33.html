In many cases I think being considered a senior developer is the start of your career, not the end (it's like finishing an apprenticeship) - but the pay scale just doesn't reflect that. Most of the people that I would say fall more towards distinguished engineer than just joe senior-developer in NZ are contracting/consulting or are at least part owners of the company they work for (a few of them attend the architecture chat ;o).... obviously based on demand you can make a reasonable living from that - but when it comes down to it, you're generally in NZ for family, lifestyle or just because it feels like home. What can a small country like NZ do? Well I think you just need to escape bespoke work - product development, for products/services that appeal to the world stage allow you to get paid in world prices, with the obvious trickle-down effect of paying better money to individual developers involved. Downside is start-up companies in NZ will struggle to find enough/any VC to make themselves viable without Angel funding. Short answer - stick to the US or UK until you need to head back to NZ for babysitters :P ---
