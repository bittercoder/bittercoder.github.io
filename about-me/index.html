I'm writing this on a rainy day in August 2012, in Auckland, New Zealand.

My Name is Alex Henderson and I'm a 32 year old software developer, who has been working professionally as a software developer since 1999.

</a>

I've had a passion for software development from a very young age - I knew I wanted to write programs for computers since I was 6 years old. My first programs were written on a borrowed Commodore Vic 20, but I really started programming heavily first in GW-Basic</a> and Quick Basic</a> on 286 12mhz clone PC when I was 7 years old.

I learnt C++ when I was 10, but didn't start getting serious about writing software in C++ until age 11 when my family upgrade their computer to a 486 dx 33mhz. During that time I was fascinated with writing 64K Demo's and games in Turbo C++</a> and in-line assembler, also making use of $5 toll call weekends to dialup to BBS's around New Zealand, shortly before switching to dial-up internet.

I did a "Bachelor of Computing Systems" degree at Unitec from age 17 to 19, and while there was working part time for Schneider Electric. After graduating I got my first full-time development job at Terabyte Interactive</a>, which was based in New Market at the time. While there I worked on ASP code, developing COM components in Visual Basic and working on some C++ projects including a 3D Visualization of a rowing machine. I also spearheaded the introduction of ASP.Net, writing code on the beta releases of C# and ASP.Net.

I eventually took redundancy from that position and next went to work next at Real Developments (also known as Real Sports) which was owned by Lion Breweries - where I stayed for a while working on email marketing solutions.

After leaving Real Development I began working for the North Shore City Council, as the lead developer there committing a lot of my time there on Asset Management solutions (including a product called AMIS), GIS and data warehousing.

</a>

After the North Shore City Council I took 6 months off and traveled around Asia</a>, before returning and taking up a position at a startup called "Syzmk" - working on a product which never quite got off the ground, but in concept was similar to technologies such as IFTTT</a>.

The start-up eventually imploded in on itself, but the friendship with the founder was not harmed in the process, which I think in retrospect was more important than the success of the idea, and the project was a great opportunity to learn new things.

Finally after spending a 6 years working full time for companies around New Zealand I felt I had gained the experience and contacts necessary to strike out on my own and start my own company - which I did, launching DevDefined Limited in 2007, which has been very successful ever since.

DevDefined Limited</a> is a boutique consultancy focusing on Product Development and Development Mentoring, which has now been operating for over 6 years. The first product we developed was Screen Architect, a UI prototyping plugin for Enterprise Architect, which was launched in 2007 and won a Jolt Productivity award in 2009</a>.

As well as running my own business (with my wife) I work on a number of open source projects periodically including:

  •  DevDefined.OAuth</a>, a popular OAuth 1.0 library, which has been incorporated into a number of products since it's introduction including the Xero Client library.</li>
  • LOB</a>, a library for managing the persistence of blobs in NHibernate in a way which allows you to switch the backing store used (i.e. Filesystem, S3, in-database etc.)</li>
  • Organising a local developer event called the "Architecture Chat</a>" - which has had over 150 meetings to date since it was started - it is one of the most successful and long-running community events for .Net developers (though all dev's are welcome) in the Auckland region.</li>
    </ul>
    In the last 3 years I have been focusing a large part of my energies in developing     Enterprise Tester</a>, a test management solution written in .Net and produced by Catch Software</a> .

    Enterprise Tester is the most complex software project I have ever started from scratch - check out my posts on the Catch blog</a> if you are interested in what we are doing! Enterprise Tester won a Consensus Software award in 2010</a> and makes heavy use of many technologies including the DevDefined.OAuth and LOB projects.

    In February 2008 I married my wife, who is my business partner in DevDefined Limited and who is also on the Board of Trustees for the New Zealand Bird Rescue Charitable Trust</a>, you can keep track of everything she is up to by reading the NZBRCT facebook page</a>, or following her updates on twitter @birdnerdnews</a> - she's also an avid amateur photographer posting photos to all the above sites, as well as flickr</a>.

    </a>

    I currently reside in Auckland with my Lovely Wife, 1 cat, 3 budgies and 1 Cockatiel and also own a funky little 100+ year old Villa in Dargaville where I like to spend time over the summer with family, swimming and of course writing code.
